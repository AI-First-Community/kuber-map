A pattern across 500 years

Every leap began with
a shared language.

1494

Double-entry bookkeeping

Luca Pacioli codifies it in print — a shared grammar for value that still underpins every ledger.

1973

SWIFT

Banks agree on one messaging standard, so money can move between institutions that don't share systems.

2012

The Legal Entity Identifier

After 2008, regulators couldn't reliably say who was who. The LEI (ISO 17442) gives every entity one global name.

2010s

FIBO

The EDM Council models the concepts — loans, securities, owners, reports — as one open, machine-readable ontology.

What this is

Kuber Map.

FIBO is rigorous but built for modelers. Kuber Map reshapes it into a teachable map and portable context packs an agent retrieves and cites — with a FIBO provenance trail for audit.

Named for Kubera, keeper of wealth. Its map is forked from Bodhi Map, the open knowledge graph of modern AI, and reimagined for finance.
One curated concept at the center; every neighbor a cited FIBO relation.
The philosophy

Three rules we don't break.

01

Teach, don't dump

A hand-picked, learner-first "core" — not the whole ontology firehosed at an expert.

02

Never blur provenance

Every fact is labeled standard or curated. You always know who is talking.

03

Audit over hype

Every concept carries its FIBO citation, so an answer is traceable — for a reviewer or a regulator.
