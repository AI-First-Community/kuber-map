A pattern across 500 years

Every leap in finance
began with a shared language.

1494

Double-entry bookkeeping

Luca Pacioli codifies it in print — a shared grammar for value that still underpins every ledger.

1973

SWIFT

Banks agree on one messaging standard so money can move between institutions that don't share systems.

2012

The Legal Entity Identifier

After 2008, regulators couldn't reliably say who was who. The LEI (ISO 17442) gives every entity one global name.

2010s

FIBO

The EDM Council models the concepts themselves — loans, securities, owners, reports — as one open, machine-readable ontology.

And now, a new participant

Finance has a new decision-maker.
It doesn't speak the language yet.

Every prior leap gave people and systems a shared, precise vocabulary. Today the newest actor on the desk is an AI agent — fluent in words, but not grounded in what a mortgage, a beneficial owner, or a regulatory report actually is.
The next shared language isn't for humans, or for machines that move money. It's for the machines that now reason about it.

The insight

Fluent is not
grounded.

A large language model talks about a mortgage or a beneficial owner convincingly — but approximately. In finance, approximately-right is the kind of wrong that passes a demo and fails an audit.
The fix isn't a bigger model. It's grounding — giving the agent the industry's precise, shared, auditable definitions, so every answer can cite its source.

What this is

Kuber Map turns FIBO into
grounding an agent can cite.

FIBO is rigorous but built for modelers — exhaustive and hard to use directly. Kuber Map reshapes it into a teachable map and into portable context packs a financial AI agent retrieves and cites, with a FIBO provenance trail for audit.
Named for Kubera, the treasurer and keeper of wealth. Its interactive map is forked from Bodhi Map — the open knowledge graph of modern AI — and reimagined for finance.
Kuber Map
The philosophy

Three rules we don't break.

01

Teach, don't dump

A hand-picked, learner-first "core" — not the whole ontology firehosed at an expert.

02

Never blur provenance

Every fact is labeled standard or curated. You always know who is talking.

03

Audit over hype

Every concept carries its FIBO citation, so an answer is traceable — for a reviewer or a regulator.

Value is measured, not claimed — and the whole thing is open, MIT-licensed, and free.

Why this is essential for agentic AI

The more autonomous the agent,
the less room to be wrong.

Agents chain steps

An agent doesn't answer once — it reasons across many hops. One wrong semantic early poisons the whole chain.

Cross-domain by nature

Real tasks span loans, securities, entities, reports. Grounding must connect domains — exactly what curated bridges do.

No human in every loop

Autonomy removes the reviewer who once caught the confident mistake. The citation trail becomes the control.

Trust is the unlock

Institutions deploy what they can prove. Grounding turns "impressive demo" into "audit-ready system."
