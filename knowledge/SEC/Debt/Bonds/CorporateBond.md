detail: "Corporate bonds are issued for purposes such as mergers and acquisitions, business expansion, or to cover ongoing operational needs, and are typically longer-term debt instruments that have a maturity of at least one year. Corporate debt instruments with maturity shorter than one year are referred to as commercial paper. Note that some classification schemes consider any bond except those issued by a government in its own currency to be a corporate bond, for example, a bond issued by Canada in US dollars might be classified as a corporate bond. Bonds issued by multinational / supranational organizations such as the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) may also be considered corporate bonds rather than government bonds."
