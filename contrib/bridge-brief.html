Six representative cross-domain relations FIBO doesn't yet draw

From 19 grounded relations surfaced while building learner-first, agent-grounding maps of FIBO across five use cases. Both endpoints are real FIBO/Commons classes; none duplicates an edge FIBO already asserts; each carries a rationale + citation. Shown in a neutral kmb: namespace — we identify the gap, not the property.

mortgage-backed security SEC backed-by mortgage LOAN
SEC ↔ LOANPooled mortgage loans back an MBS; FIBO models both, but draws no edge from the security to the loans that back it. SEC Regulation AB · 17 CFR 229.1100
asset-backed security SEC backed-by loan LOAN
SEC ↔ LOANAn ABS is collateralized by a pool of receivables such as consumer or auto loans; the backing relationship is left implicit. SEC Regulation AB · 17 CFR 229.1100
reporting party FND identified-by legal entity identifier BE
FND ↔ BEThe party that submits a supervisory or transaction report is identified by its LEI; FIBO defines both, but not the link. ISO 17442 (LEI) · MiFID II / CFTC reporting
credit default swap DER references bond SEC
DER ↔ SECA CDS transfers the credit risk of a reference obligation — typically a bond of the reference entity — but FIBO draws no edge from the swap to it. ISDA Credit Derivatives Definitions
regulatory report CMNS submitted-to regulatory agency CMNS
intra-domainA report is submitted to the agency that requires it; Commons models both classes but not the filing edge between them. Supervisory practice · HMDA→CFPB, MiFID II→NCAs
beneficial owner BE beneficial-owner-of legal entity CMNS
BE ↔ CMNSThe party that ultimately owns or controls a legal entity — core to UBO resolution; FIBO models the role but draws no edge to the entity owned. FATF Recommendations 24 & 25 · 31 CFR 1010.230
